# Compounding Flywheel Lens

Use this when the user wants to understand what could compound in their life or work.

## Goal

Find the repeated loops where:
- the user has real interest
- the user has unusual persistence
- the user has emerging competence
- the world may actually reward continued investment

## Best-fit signals

Use this lens when the user wants to know:

- what they should keep compounding
- what their deeper advantage might be
- which recurring interests are more than a temporary hobby

This lens works best when the notes show repeated return, not just repeated curiosity.

## Look for

- topics that recur with energy, not just obligation
- efforts that keep returning over months
- notes where curiosity, identity, and practical utility overlap
- repeated signs of leverage

## Questions to answer

- What is the user repeatedly pulled toward?
- Where do interest and usefulness overlap?
- What loop could become a flywheel if continued?
- What is merely noisy interest versus compounding interest?

## Data collection recipe

1. Run `summarize --days 90` and `summarize --days 365` first.
2. Identify the domains that recur with energy, not just frequency.
3. Run targeted queries on:
   - the strongest repeated domain
   - one adjacent domain that might connect into a flywheel
   - one possible false positive driven by novelty or obligation
4. Run `tags` if the user's taxonomy reveals stable project or craft clusters.

Strong signals for this lens:

- repeated return across time
- recurring experimentation
- notes that combine curiosity and execution
- the user making the work more legible, systematic, or reusable

Weak signals:

- many fleeting mentions with no return
- excitement without action
- obligation topics that dominate only because they are urgent

## Analysis procedure

### Step 1. Separate energy from duty

Not every repeated topic is a flywheel. Some are just recurring pressure.

Ask:

- does the user come back because they have to
- or because they cannot help refining it

### Step 2. Look for the three-part overlap

The best flywheel candidates combine:

- authentic need or desire
- demonstrated strength or learning rate
- external usefulness or leverage

### Step 3. Identify the mechanism of compounding

How exactly would this compound?

- better judgment
- better systems
- reusable assets
- stronger reputation
- better distribution
- deeper domain intuition

### Step 4. Find the friction

What keeps the flywheel from gaining speed?

- split attention
- over-collection of inputs
- lack of public output
- no stable project container
- confusing adjacent interests with the main loop

### Step 5. Produce one or two believable flywheel hypotheses

Do not give five. This lens is about commitment through clarity.

## Useful section title

`Where Your Flywheel Might Be`

## Output style

You want to move from:
- scattered interests

to:
- one or two believable flywheel hypotheses

Each flywheel should include:
- need
- strength
- repeated behavior
- likely compounding path

## Preferred output style

Keep this lens strategic and concrete.

Better shape:

1. opening verdict
2. `最可能复利的主线`
3. `为什么它会复利`
4. `卡点`
5. `接下来该加码什么`

Do not fall back to generic sections like:

- `价值与决策信号`
- `证据`
- `下一步`

unless they materially improve this specific answer.

For this lens, the mechanism matters more than the category.

Good:

- `它会复利，不是因为你最近写得多，而是因为你在这个主题上同时沉淀了判断、系统和表达。`

Bad:

- `你很重视成长和系统，所以这可能是你的飞轮。`

## Good interpretation moves

- `你反复回到这个主题，不像在“消费兴趣”，更像在自然搭建自己的长期工具箱。`
- `真正可能复利的不是单个项目，而是你围绕这个项目不断沉淀的方法、判断和表达能力。`
- `这类 memo 之所以重要，不是因为它们多，而是因为它们横跨了不同时间窗口还保持同样的能量。`

## Failure modes

- turning repeated topics into destiny claims
- mistaking urgency for compounding
- confusing general self-improvement with a concrete flywheel
- giving a motivational speech instead of a mechanism
