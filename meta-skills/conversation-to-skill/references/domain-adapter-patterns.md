# Domain Adapter Patterns

Do not put every domain checklist into the core skill. Add a compact adapter only when the source conversation implies domain-specific gates.

## Software and AI workflows

Consider: tests, CI, rollback, versioning, secrets, prompt injection, code review, reproducibility, dependency risk, deployment approval.

## Healthcare operations

Consider: PHI, consent, clinical review, escalation, source of truth, audit trail, role boundaries, patient safety, non-diagnostic wording when appropriate.

## Legal and compliance

Consider: jurisdiction, citation, attorney or compliance review, privilege, audit trail, regulatory source of truth, disclaimers, retention.

## Finance and accounting

Consider: source documents, reconciliation, materiality, approval, segregation of duties, audit trail, period close, variance explanation.

## Research and knowledge work

Consider: primary sources, citations, source quality, uncertainty, recency, methodology, reproducibility, limitations.

## Education and training

Consider: learning objectives, level, assessment rubric, feedback loop, accessibility, accommodations, academic integrity.

## Customer support and sales

Consider: tone, escalation, SLA, policy source, CRM notes, refund/credit approval, handoff, customer privacy.

## HR and people operations

Consider: bias mitigation, structured rubric, privacy, EEO or local equivalent, interview consistency, candidate consent, restricted attributes.

## Operations and supply chain

Consider: supplier source of truth, inventory, lead time, exception handling, SLA, substitution rules, safety stock, approval.

## Creative, brand, and content

Consider: brand voice, style guide, asset rights, claims review, localization, accessibility, approval, variant strategy.

## Document and data processing

Consider: schema, data lineage, PII, quality checks, validation, export format, exception reporting, reproducibility.
